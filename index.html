The Shame Layer

What You Were Taught to Do With This.

You were taught to keep moving. To not let them see you struggle. To be the one who holds it together — for your family, your team, your community. That training is in your body too. And so when the body finally responds — when you freeze, or snap, or sleep for twelve hours — it doesn't just feel hard.

It feels like failure.

It is not failure. It is your body finally telling the truth. This is where we put that script down.

"You were never supposed to break. You were just supposed to keep going. That was never a fair ask."

03
Expanding the Map

What Your Body Has Been Doing — And Why.

In The Body Remembers, you named four survival responses — Fight, Flight, Freeze, and Fawn. That was the beginning of the map. And it was true.

But your survival has never been that simple. Two responses were missing from that map — and they matter, especially for Black women navigating systems that were never built for them.

We are expanding the map now. Because you deserve language for everything your body has been doing — not just part of it.

Select every response you recognize in yourself right now.

Freeze Going Still "You aren't cowardly because you didn't email that contact or reach out to that potential resource." That is a Freeze response. Your system decided that silence is safer than a 'no.' It is protecting you from more rejection.
Fight On Edge "You aren't a bad mom, friend, or partner because you've been snappy or short-fused." That is your Fight response. Your body feels attacked by the uncertainty of the unknown, and it's trying to defend your territory.
Justice Wanting Accountability "You aren't 'bitter' or 'difficult' for wanting to report that company or seek legal action." Your sense of justice is a functional part of you. You are identifying a breach of safety that needs resolution. This isn't bitterness — this is your body refusing to pretend the harm wasn't real.
Flight Racing Forward "You aren't 'manic' because you're applying to 50 jobs a day or staying up until 3AM on your business plan." That is a Flight response. You are literally trying to fly away from something that feels unsafe. You're racing toward a landing strip.
Fawn Over-Extending "You aren't lacking backbone because you're agreeing to low-paying projects just to feel like you have a safety net." This is your Fawn response. Your system is trying to create security the only way it knows how right now.
Forced Recovery Shutting Down "You are certainly not lazy for sleeping the day away." That is your body's way of Forced Recovery. You have been running on high-octane cortisol, and your system is finally demanding a shutdown to prevent a total collapse.
"Your system is not against you. It never was. And now you have more names for what it has been doing."

Regulation Activity

Moving the Energy Through.

To move from survival to strategy, we don't try harder. We regulate. Work through each step in order. Take your time.

✓
Intentional Breathwork
1 minute

Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, out for 6. Let your exhale be longer than your inhale. This signals safety to your nervous system.

1:00
Somatic Movement
1 minute

Shake your hands, roll your shoulders, stretch your neck. Let the body move however it needs to. You are shifting the energy out of stillness.

1:00
The Fear Dump
3 minutes

Every fear onto the page. Don't edit. Don't pause. Just write until the timer stops. Then go back and circle — or type here — the words and fears that appear more than once.
