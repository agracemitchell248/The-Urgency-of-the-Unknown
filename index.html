02
Building on The Body Remembers

Your Body Is Not Malfunctioning

In The Body Remembers, you learned that your body holds stress as physical memory — the clenched jaw, the tight shoulders, the pit in your stomach. You named what was living there. That was the foundation.

Now we go deeper. Because survival doesn't show up as just one thing. It shows up in patterns — and each pattern has been trying to keep you safe in its own way.

In The Body Remembers, you named four survival responses — Fight, Flight, Freeze, and Fawn. That was the beginning of the map. And it was true.

But your survival has never been that simple. Two responses were missing from that map — and they matter, especially for Black women navigating systems that were never built for them.

We are expanding the map now. Because you deserve language for everything your body has been doing — not just part of it.

The Freeze Response
Going Still
"You aren't cowardly because you didn't email that contact or reach out to that potential resource."
That is a Freeze response. Your system decided that silence is safer than a 'no.' It is protecting you from more rejection.
The Fight Response
On Edge
"You aren't a bad mom, friend, or partner because you've been snappy or short-fused."
That is your Fight response. Your body feels attacked by the uncertainty of the unknown, and it's trying to defend your territory.
New in This Chapter
The Justice Response
Wanting Accountability
"You aren't 'bitter' or 'difficult' for wanting to report that company or seek legal action."
Your sense of justice is a functional part of you. You are identifying a breach of safety that needs resolution. This isn't bitterness — this is your body refusing to pretend the harm wasn't real.
The Flight Response
Racing Forward
"You aren't 'manic' because you're applying to 50 jobs a day or staying up until 3AM on your business plan."
That is a Flight response. You are literally trying to fly away from something that feels unsafe. You're racing toward a landing strip.
The Fawn Response
Over-Extending
"You aren't lacking backbone because you're agreeing to low-paying projects just to feel like you have a safety net."
This is your Fawn response. Your system is trying to create security the only way it knows how right now.
New in This Chapter
The Recovery Response
Shutting Down
"You are certainly not lazy for sleeping the day away."
That is your body's way of Forced Recovery. You have been running on high-octane cortisol, and your system is finally demanding a shutdown to prevent a total collapse.

"Your system is not against you. It never was. And now you have more names for what it has been doing."

04
Regulation Activity

Break the Spike

To get from survival to strategy, we have to break that spike. Not by trying harder. Not by pushing through. We do it through regulation.

"You don't get to audacity from cortisol. You get there from here — from your own regulated body."

1
1-Minute Intentional Breathwork
Breathe in slowly for 4 counts. Hold for 4. Exhale for 6. Let each exhale be a release. Use the timer below.
1:00
2
1-Minute Somatic Movement
Roll your shoulders. Shake out your hands. Roll your neck slowly. Let your body move the energy that language cannot. This is not exercise — it is release.
1:00
3
3-Minute Fear Dump
Set the timer. Write every fear you have — without editing, without judgment. Get it out of your body and onto the page. When you're done, go back and circle any words that appear more than once. Your patterns are in there.
3:00

This stays with you. Come back and circle the repeated words when you're done.
